The problem

Nigeria's healthcare system loses time exactly where time matters most.

Lafia starts from a painful truth: patients often do not suffer because expertise is absent, but because information is fragmented. In emergencies, referrals, chronic care, and public hospitals, missing context directly becomes delayed treatment, wasted money, and preventable harm.

01

🏥No shared records across hospitals

Every new facility restarts intake from zero. History is retold, investigations are repeated, and costs compound for the patient.

02

📁Paper folders create duplication by design

At LASUTH, a patient already registered still had a second folder opened and another registration fee charged. The workflow is the bug.

03

🚑Unconscious patients cannot advocate for themselves

Drug allergies, blood type, chronic conditions, and current medications are often invisible exactly when emergency teams need them most.

04

🔒No audit trail means no real accountability

If sensitive records are viewed or leaked, tracing who accessed them is difficult. Compliance cannot rely on trust alone.

Case study
The tumour nobody knew about.

A patient was admitted with blood sugar above 600 mg/dL. Doctors treated the numbers in front of them because that was the information available in the room.

What they did not know was that years earlier, at a different hospital, imaging had already identified a pituitary tumour that could explain the crisis. That scan lived in a folder somewhere else.

Lafia's value proposition becomes immediate here: the right doctor should not need luck, memory, or family availability to find critical prior history.

The solution

One identity. One record. Accountable always.

Lafia is a unified health identity platform anchored to the National Identification Number. It gives every patient a portable longitudinal record and gives every verified institution a shared accountability layer for care, access, and traceability.

Step 1

🆔Patient identity is created once

A patient registers with NIN and receives a lifelong health identity, rather than a new hospital-specific record every time.

Step 2

🏥Institutions are verified and onboarded

Hospitals, clinics, labs, and pharmacies plug into the same trust layer with role-based permissions.

Step 3

🧾Every interaction becomes part of the record

Visits, diagnoses, prescriptions, imaging, and lab results are logged into a single longitudinal view.

Step 4

🚨Emergency mode resolves identity fast

Fingerprint, card, QR, proxy, family lookup, or temporary anonymous record keep treatment moving without losing data.

Step 5

🛡️Every access is logged and attributable

Audit trails and invisible watermarking turn sensitive medical access into something measurable and enforceable.

Emergency scenario

When a patient is brought in unconscious, Lafia is designed to keep treatment moving.

This is one of Lafia's most important stories and it deserves to be explicit on the page. The system is built around a simple principle: identity resolution must help treatment, never delay it. If the patient cannot speak, the platform still works through a layered fallback chain.

Use case

🚑 A patient arrives after a road accident, unconscious and unable to answer a single question.

⚠️ The emergency team cannot wait for paperwork or perfect certainty.

In today's reality, this is where dangerous guesswork begins. The doctor may not know the patient's blood type, severe allergies, current medications, chronic conditions, or who to call. Under pressure, the hospital starts reconstructing identity while treatment time slips away.

🩸 First, the team needs only the essentials. Blood type, drug allergies, active medications, chronic conditions, and emergency contact details are surfaced in a stripped-down emergency view.
💊 Second, the system helps prevent the wrong intervention. If the patient has a documented severe reaction to a common antibiotic or another contraindication, that warning is visible before treatment is administered.
📝 Third, nothing is lost even if identity is not confirmed immediately. A temporary anonymous emergency record captures drugs given, observations made, and treatment notes in real time until the patient is matched and merged.
Fallback chain

Six ways Lafia can identify the patient without a single point of failure.

1
👆 Fingerprint scanner

Emergency desk hardware checks the patient's fingerprint against identity infrastructure and pulls the Lafia record instantly when there is a match.

2
🪪 Physical Lafia card

A wallet card with NIN and QR gives staff a fast backup when the patient has no phone or their phone is inaccessible.

3
📞 Medical proxy contact

If direct lookup fails, the pre-registered emergency contact can confirm identity and authorize emergency access, with that action logged.

4
🙂 Face recognition

A later-phase fallback can compare the patient's face with identity records to return NIN even when they remain unresponsive.

5
👨‍👩‍👧 Family manual NIN entry

If relatives are present and know the patient's NIN, staff can enter it manually and access the emergency record immediately.

6
🧷 Temporary anonymous record

If every other method fails, care still starts. Lafia creates a temporary record and automatically merges it once identity is confirmed.

✅ The core promise: treatment is never blocked waiting for identity, and medical context is surfaced as early as possible.
